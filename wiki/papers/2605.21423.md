# Temperature-induced optical enhancement near a localization transition

**Autori:** Raul Liquito et al. · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21423) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.21423)

## Abstract

This study examines optical conductivity in quasiperiodic systems, intermediate between periodic crystals and disordered systems. Using the Aubry-André model, researchers find quasiperiodic potentials restructure low-frequency optical signals, with optical gaps closing discontinuously near metal-insulator transitions. A mechanism for strong low-frequency finite temperature optical conductivity enhancement at resonant frequencies stems from thermal activation of Pauli-blocked transitions between van Hove singularities.
