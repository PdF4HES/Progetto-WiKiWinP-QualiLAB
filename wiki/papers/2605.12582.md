# On the Anticipation of Lunar Travel in the Early 20th Century: A Pedagogical Exercise

**Autori:** Tina A. Harriott, Cherif F. Matta · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12582) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.12582)

## Abstract

This article examines from historical and pedagogical perspectives Alphonse Berget's anticipation of Earth-Moon travel in Le Ciel (Larousse, 1923). Unlike Jules Verne's fictional treatment, Berget approached space travel using physical reasoning grounded in Newtonian gravitation. Using qualitative and semi-quantitative arguments, he identified principal Earth-Moon trajectory phases and estimated travel time of approximately 49 hours, comparable to Apollo mission transit times.
