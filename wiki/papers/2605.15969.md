# Quantum mechanics for classical transport equations

**Autori:** Christof Wetterich · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15969) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15969)

## Abstract

Classical transport equations with probabilistic initial conditions are formulated as quantum systems. Discrete versions correspond to probabilistic automata where time-local information encodes in a classical wave function undergoing unitary evolution following a Schrödinger equation. Statistical observables are represented by non-commuting operators. A complex functional integral describes the quantum system, demonstrating how quantum mechanics characteristics — superposition, interference, phases, non-commutation, unitary evolution — emerge from probabilistic classical transport.
