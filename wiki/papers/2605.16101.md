# Quantum Measurement without Ontology

**Autori:** Richard Healey · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16101) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.16101)

## Abstract

No-go theorems demonstrate that quantum observable measurement differs fundamentally from classical measurement: recorded outcomes are typically created rather than revealed. Additional no-go theorems show that unitary quantum theory cannot generally explain a unique recorded outcome. Nevertheless, methodological norms inherent in quantum physical practice establish the objectivity of unique recorded measurement outcomes and non-quantum world features that physicists take their models to represent.
