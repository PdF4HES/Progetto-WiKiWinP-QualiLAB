# EASE Configuration Facilitates A Reproducible Science of LLM Social Simulations

**Autori:** Sneheel Sarangi, Maximilian Puelma Touzel, Aurélien Bück-Kaeffer et al. · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30258) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.30258)

## Abstract

LLMs are increasingly deployed to simulate social interactions, yet existing simulators remain ad hoc and monolithic. This work modularizes core components into Environments, Agents, Simulation engines, and Evaluation metrics (EASE). SiliSocS, an open-source research-ready Silicon Society Sandbox, implements study-structured EASE configuration enabling highly configurable and reproducible LLM-based social simulations. Three case studies showcase the system's comprehensive assessment, ability to dive deeper into complex questions, and elaboration of existing studies.
