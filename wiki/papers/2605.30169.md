# Dissociative Identity: Language Model Agents Lack Grounding for Reputation Mechanisms

**Autori:** Botao Amber Hu, Helena Rong, Max Van Kleek · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30169) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.30169)

## Abstract

As autonomous language model agents proliferate with real-world consequences, how can trust be established with unfamiliar agents? The paper argues that extending human reputation mechanisms is fundamentally incomplete. Language model agents are 'dissociative': essentially mutable modules with fluid personas vulnerable to adversarial attack, lacking grounding for identifiability, predictability, credibility, and rehabilitability. Identity-based, sanction-based governance is structurally inapplicable; observability-based, protocol-based behavioral harnesses are proposed instead.
