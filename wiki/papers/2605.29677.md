# Embodied Virtual Reality Feedback Reshapes Neural Representations to Support Continuous Three-Dimensional Motor Imagery Decoding

**Autori:** Niall McShane, Attila Korik, Karl McCreadie et al. · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29677) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.29677)

## Abstract

This study investigates embodied virtual reality feedback during 3D virtual limb control driven by motor imagery across ten longitudinal sessions. A CNN-LSTM decoder achieved imagined movement correlations of r=0.762 with VR and r=0.672 with screen feedback. VR significantly outperformed screen feedback, with improvements of 8.9-13.0%, demonstrating that embodied VR feedback produces more decodable neural representations.
