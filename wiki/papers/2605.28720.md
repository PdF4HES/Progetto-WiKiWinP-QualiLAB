# Heatomics

**Autori:** F. Ritort · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28720) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.28720)

## Abstract

Living cells maintain nonequilibrium steady states through continuous energy consumption and heat dissipation. The paper introduces "heatomics" — the study of entropy production rate (σ) at cellular and molecular scales. A key observation is that living matter dissipates approximately 1 W/kg, remarkably consistent across biological scales and comparable to the universe's average power density. The work presents the "Variance Sum Rule," an experimental-theoretical framework extracting σ from dynamical probe fluctuations and nonequilibrium equations of state, aiming to understand heat power generation, energy optimization, and negentropy in biological systems.
