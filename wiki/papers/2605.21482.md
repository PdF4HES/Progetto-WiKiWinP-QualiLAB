# DeepWeb-Bench: A Deep Research Benchmark Demanding Massive Cross-Source Evidence and Long-Horizon Derivation

**Autori:** Sixiong Xie et al. · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21482) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.21482)

## Abstract

Deep research, in which an agent searches the open web, collects evidence, and derives an answer through extended reasoning, is a prominent use case for frontier language models. DeepWeb-Bench is introduced as a substantially harder benchmark than existing ones. Difficulty comes from three properties: each task requires massive evidence collection, cross-source reconciliation, and long-horizon multi-step derivation. Results from nine frontier models show retrieval is not the bottleneck; derivation and calibration failures account for over 70% of errors.
