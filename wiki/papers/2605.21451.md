# Approximation Theory for Neural Networks: Old and New

**Autori:** Soumendu Sundar Mukherjee, Himasish Talukdar · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21451) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.21451)

## Abstract

This survey examines universal approximation theorems that explain neural networks' expressive power, asserting feedforward networks are dense in broad function classes. The work evolves qualitative universality results into quantitative theory addressing approximation rates, parameter efficiency, and architectural features like depth and width. Emphasis is placed on depth-width trade-offs demonstrating deeper architectures achieve superior parameter efficiency for structured functions. Recent developments on Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks are also reviewed as alternative architectural paradigms.
