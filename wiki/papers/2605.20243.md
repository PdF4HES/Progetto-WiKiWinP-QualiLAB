# God numbers for Graphs, Games and Groups

**Autori:** Z. Adams et al. · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20243) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.20243)

## Abstract

Describes and axiomatizes finite solitaire puzzles and zero-sum sequential games using graph theory. The god number quantifies moves necessary to solve puzzles, representing minimal distance from initial state to solution space. Examples include Rubik-type problems, sliding puzzles, and two-player games, with god number computation shown to be NP-complete.
