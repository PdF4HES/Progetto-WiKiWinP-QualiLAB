# A note on the Alon-Saks-Seymour problem

**Autori:** Jacob Fox · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28915) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.28915)

## Abstract

Let f(k) be the maximum possible chromatic number of a graph whose edge set can be partitioned into at most k complete bipartite graphs. Alon, Saks, and Seymour conjectured that f(k)=k+1 for all k. While the conjecture was verified for k ≤ 9 by Gao et al., it was disproved by Huang and Sudakov, and further Balodis et al. proved that f(k) ≥ 2^{Ω̃((log k)^2)}. In this note, we give a simple proof of the recursive upper bound f(k+1) ≤ f(k)+f(⌊k/4⌋). Consequently, f(k) ≤ 2^{(log_2 (4k))^2/4} for k ≥ 1. This improves the previous best known upper bound in the exponent by a factor which is asymptotically two.
