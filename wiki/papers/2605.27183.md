# 2-word-π-representable Graphs

**Autori:** Duncan Adamson, Amanita Dietz, Pamela Fleischmann et al. · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27183) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.27183)

## Abstract

This paper investigates the new notion of 2-word-π-representable graphs: the nodes of the graph correspond to the letters of the two words and there exists an edge between two nodes if the projections of any two letters of both words are equal. The benefit of not only using one word for a representation is that every graph is 2-word-π-representable. We present an algorithm that returns two representing words for any graph. Aside, we show that every permutation graph is representable by two 1-uniform words and give constructions how graph operations on 2-word-π-representable graphs can be realised on their representing words.
