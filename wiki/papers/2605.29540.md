# Dichotomy study of the Steiner tree problem in split-like graphs

**Autori:** Jyothish S, Sadagopan Narasimhan · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29540) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.29540)

## Abstract

Given a connected graph G and a terminal set R ⊆ V(G), the minimum Steiner tree problem (ST) asks for a tree that spans all of R with at most r vertices from V(G)\R. We introduce the class of split-like graphs which unifies several known graph classes like bipartite graphs, split graphs, and bisplit graphs. We investigate the computational complexity of the Steiner tree problem under structural constraints, specifically K_{1,r}-free, bounded diameter, chordality and star-convexity. Through reductions from Exact-3-Cover and its variants, the paper establishes a series of dichotomy results. The boundary for K_{1,r}-free bipartite graphs is established: ST is in P for r ≤ 3 and NP-complete for r ≥ 4; whereas on K_{1,r}-free bisplit graphs, ST is in P for any fixed r≥ 3.
