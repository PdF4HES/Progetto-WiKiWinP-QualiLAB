# Interaction-Phase Dynamics and Spectral Organization in Damped Higher-Order Nonlinear Schrödinger Models

**Autori:** C.M. Schober · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26811) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.26811)

## Abstract

The paper investigates dynamical mechanisms in damped higher-order nonlinear Schrödinger systems. The researchers derive a five-mode carrier-sideband truncation and use amplitude-phase variables to analyze dominant four-wave interactions. They find that viscous damping acts modewise without directly modifying interaction-phase dynamics, while nonlinear mean-flow damping contributes dissipative corrections. The study uses finite-gap NLS benchmark solutions to interpret results, showing that nonlinear mean-flow damping maintains organized Floquet evolution with persistent recurrent carrier-sideband focusing, whereas viscous damping produces progressively diffuse modulation dynamics together with repeated Floquet reconfiguration and weakening recurrence.
