# Causality and Scientific Inquiry: Lessons from Space Physics and Medical Sciences

**Autori:** Marzieh Asgari-Targhi et al. · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11420) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.11420)

## Abstract

Over the past two decades, rapid expansion of data-intensive computational techniques may have diminished the use of applied mathematics in causal scientific inquiry. This paper assesses hazards of neglecting applied mathematics in favor of statistical modeling alone. Using examples from space physics and medical sciences, the work examines causal questions and demonstrates how applied mathematical and statistical methods differentiate between mechanistic and difference-making causality.
