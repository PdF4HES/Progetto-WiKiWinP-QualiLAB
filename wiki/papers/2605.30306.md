# On abelian periodicity of purely morphic words

**Autori:** Arina Filimonova, Svetlana Puzynina · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30306) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.30306)

## Abstract

Deciding periodicity of infinite words generated by morphisms is a classical result in combinatorics on words from 80's by Harju, Linna and Pansiot. In this paper, we are interested in this question in the abelian setting. Two words are called abelian equivalent if they contain the same numbers of occurrences of each letter. An infinite word s is called ultimately abelian periodic if it can be factorized as s=uv_1v_2v_3⋯, where v_i's are abelian equivalent words. We provide a characterization of binary morphisms generating abelian periodic words: A word generated by a binary morphism f is abelian periodic if and only if either it is periodic or there exist an integer K and words u, v, u', v' such that f^K(a) = uv, f^K(b) = u'v', u∼_{ab} u', and vu and v'u' are abelian periodic with abelian equivalent periods. We also provide an upper bound on K which makes the obtained characterization algorithmic.
