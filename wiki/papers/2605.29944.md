# Quadratic Sums-of-Powers for Fixed-Parameter Tractable Quantum-Circuit Simulation

**Autori:** Alexis de Colnet, Floris Geerts, Rihan Hai et al. · **2026** · [ArXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29944) · [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.29944)

## Abstract

Strongly simulating a quantum circuit, that is, computing an output amplitude, amounts to summing the circuit's Feynman paths, a weighted count over assignments to the Boolean path variables. We show that the structural quantity most accurately governing the difficulty is the rank-width of the path-variable graph, and we give an algorithm that evaluates the amplitude in time that is exponential only in this rank-width and polynomial in the circuit size. Rank-width can be far smaller than the widths that control competing methods. The new method applies to every circuit built from Hadamard and diagonal gates, in particular to circuits over Clifford+T.
